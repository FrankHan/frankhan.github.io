简单，是一种生活方式

Posted on 2018-01-08 | In Diary , Reading |

简单，快乐！

Repost from Source

I find the less I have, the more free I feel.

No, this isn’t some spiritual talk I’m about to give you.

But, I have become more and more minimalist over the last year.

Let me tell you 5 benefits of living a minimalist lifestyle and what you’ll gain from my point of view.

You’ll feel more free to get up and move.

I currently work remotely from my parent’s house in California.

Before moving back, I was living in South Carolina and had a 1 bedroom apartment.

However, even there, I always felt like it was too much space for me.

I could easily have done with just a studio. There were some pros: 5 minute walk to work.

Without having a car, it was the most optimal place to live.

When I moved back, I got rid of everything i owned down to just 3 bags of stuff.

And guess what? 95% of that was clothes.

To date, my possessions include: clothes, my laptops (work and personal), my headphones, and my cellphone.

I don’t want anything more. In fact, I’d actually like to get rid of some more of my clothes.

When you get rid of the crap you don’t need, you actually feel more liberated to up and move, as opposed to the masses that have a myriad of subscriptions, a house, cars, etc.

In essence, most people are walking debt-mines.

You’ll feel less connected to materialism.

This is by far one of the greatest reasons to simplify your life.

I don’t personally own a TV (my parents do) but even they pay for the bare minimum cable.

The only thing I like to watch are documentaries and sometimes a movie on Amazon Prime.

When I first moved to South Carolina, I remember going out on the weekends and just driving around, partly because I was bored.

But, it was like I was pre-programmed to go out and buy shit I didn’t need.

It was fucking pathetic and really freaked me out.

This quickly came to an end when I realized what I was doing, but damn did that make me really think about what a lot of people do on the weekend.

Don’t believe me? Go into a mall any day of the week. This is consumerism and gluttony at its finest.

In summary: don’t buy stuff for unnecessary reasons. Buy clothes because you’re cold. Buy food because you’re hungry.

You’ll feel less reliant on your stuff.

I’m not the biggest fan of Fight Club, the movie, but one of the topics Brad Pitt talks about is how we are not what we own, etc.

This strongly resonates with me and I feel that the less stuff I have, the more disconnected I become to it.

I have the essentials: I have a computer that allows me to work. I have clothes, and I have food for nourishment.

I do not take my paycheck and money and blow it on futile junk that will make you happy for 10 minutes, after which you need to buy more to fill the void.

Guess what? Materialism is just another drug, that’s why it’s a never-ending cycle and why so many people are addicts to buying more stuff they don’t need.

Finally, you will gain greater focus on what you like.

This is more of an intrinsic benefit, but it will happen.

By having so few possessions, it’s inevitable that you will hone in on what you truly like and need.

You will discard the junk (figuratively and literally) from your life and the remains will be your true possessions.

Work hard, play hard.

A minimalist lifestyle doesn’t mean stoicism nor am I preaching that.

I advocate simplifying to enjoy your life more. By owning fewer things, you will have less stress and therefore will be more content about your life overall.

How have you simplified your life in 2017? How do you plan to simplify it in 2018?
Let me know in the comments.

Until next time,

Theo
